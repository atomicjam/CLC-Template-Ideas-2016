Governance of College

College is incorporated by Royal Charter which sets out the framework for the governance and management of College by Council, the governing body. Members of Council perform a voluntary, non-executive role in overseeing the day-to-day running of College by the Principal and her Leadership Team. College is a Registered Charity and members of Council are the trustees of the Charity.

Council comprises a maximum of 15 Elected Members appointed by the Corporate Members of College and three Nominated Members appointed by the academic staff, the support staff and the Incorporated Guild of Cheltenham Ladies’ College respectively. Members are appointed for a term of three years and may serve a maximum of three three-year terms. Each member brings a wealth of different skills and experience to Council.

Council meets once a term with an additional annual strategy meeting. Council business is carried out within a structured framework of committees and individual responsibilities relating to specific areas of College’s operation. Each committee meets at least once a term, reports termly to, and has its decisions ratified by, full Council.

Council periodically establishes committees and working groups to provide oversight of specific areas of College’s operation. These committees and working groups may include co-opted members and employees of College.

Governance and administrative support is provided to Council by the Clerk to Council and her administrative assistant.

Ms Libby Bassett – Chairman of Council

Libby retired as a partner of KPMG in the UK in 2015, although she continues to do some consultancy work for them. Libby is a qualified Chartered Accountant and has held a number of global business development roles throughout her career with KPMG.

Libby obtained her MA from the University of Cambridge. She was elected to Council in 2011 and is a former pupil. As well as being Chairman of Council, Libby is a member of the Finance and Executive Committee, the Land and Buildings Committee and the Nominations Committee.

Mr Robert Leechman – Vice Chairman of Council

Robert is Non-Executive Chairman of a London-based property development company and works with major consumer goods companies through his own consultancy. He previously held senior positions at The Coca-Cola Company and Mars Confectionery.

Robert graduated with a BA Hons in Modern History from Magdalen College, Oxford. He was elected to Council in 2011. In addition to being Vice Chairman of Council and Chairman of the Land and Buildings Committee, Robert also sits on the Finance and Executive Committee and the Nominations Committee. He is a former parent.

Ms Sally Boyle – Chairman of Nominations Committee

Sally is HR Director for Goldman Sachs International, having previously been a partner in employment law at a national law firm.

Sally is a former pupil and holds a degree from the University of Cambridge, and postgraduate qualifications from the College of Law, London and University of Leicester. She was elected to Council 2010 and is Chairman of the Nominations Committee.

Mr Gerard Evans – Nominated Safeguarding Council Member

Gerard has been the Director of Curriculum at Eton College since 2009, having previously been Head of Modern Languages and Universities Officer. Prior to his career in education, he worked for eight years in the insurance industry.

Gerard holds an MA from the University of Oxford. He was elected to Council in 2012 and is Vice Chairman of the Nominations Committee. Gerard is also the Nominated Safeguarding Council Member.

Mr Michael Liu

Michael is the managing partner of a leading international legal practice based in Hong Kong. He has served in a number of elected and appointed positions in professional, regulatory and education establishments in Hong Kong.

Michael holds a law degree from the London School of Economics. He was elected to Council in 2011 and is a current parent. Michael sits on the Finance and Executive Committee.

Mr David Pittaway QC

David is a practising barrister who has held a number senior positions in the legal profession. He is the Reader of the Inner Temple in 2016. He is a past chairman of the Bar Council’s Education and Training Committee and Social Mobility Committee. He is a former member of Alan Milburn’s Working Party on Access to the Professions. He is a former trustee of the National Education Trust.

David has an MA from the University of Cambridge. He was elected to Council in 2012 and is a member of the Development Sub-Committee.

Mrs Alison Ravano

Alison has a background in fundraising and event organising, having worked with several international charities and independent schools. Prior to this, she worked in investment banking, specialising in the corporate finance of US firms.

Alison holds a BBA and an MBA from the University of Houston and is a trustee of the Cheetah Conservation Fund UK. She was elected to Council in 2010, is a member of the Development Steering Committee, the Nominations Committee and a former parent.

Mrs Katie Robyns – Guild Representative Member of Council

Katie is the Guild Nominated Member on Council. She is a qualified barrister and is a company secretary. She has been heavily involved with fundraising and development for schools for many years.

Katie obtained her LLB from the University of Birmingham. She was elected to Council 2012 and is a member of the Finance and Executive Committee and of the Land and Buildings Committee, as well as a member of the Development Sub-Committee.

Mr Kevin Senior – Chairman of the Development Sub-Committee

Kevin is a former partner in an international services firm. He is currently Vice Chair of a local civic society, Honorary Treasurer to Art Fund Oxford and a Young Enterprise business advisor. He is a former member of the South Electricity Consumers Council.

Kevin gained a LLB from the University of Exeter and qualified as a Chartered Accountant. He was elected to Council in 2013. As well as being Chairman of the Development Sub-Committee, Kevin is also a member of the Finance and Executive Committee and the Investments Sub-Committee.

Mrs Louise Terry

Louise has spent her career managing the reputations of some of the world’s leading businesses. She started her communications career in agencies, and was a board director at Hill and Knowlton before moving in-house to become Head of Communications for Coca-Cola and then Group Communications Director for L’Oréal UK.

For ten years, Louise was Deputy Chair of Save the Children, a leading children’s charity.

Louise holds an MA in Modern Languages from the University of Cambridge. She is a former pupil of College and a current parent. She joined Council in 2011 and is a member of the Finance and Executive Committee and of the Development Sub-Committee.

Mrs Su-Mei Thompson

Su-Mei is CEO of The Women’s Foundation and a member of the Equal Opportunities Commission in Hong Kong, having previously held senior management positions at The Walt Disney Company, the Financial Times and Christie’s.

A passionate advocate for women’s advancement, Su-Mei holds MAs in Law from the University of Cambridge and the University of Oxford and an MBA from IMD in Lausanne where she was the first woman to graduate on the Dean’s List. She is a qualified solicitor and practised as a corporate lawyer at Linklaters for several years.

A former pupil, Head of House and College Prefect, Su-Mei was elected to Council in 2013. She is also a current parent.

Mr Denis Tinsley – Chairman of Development Steering Committee

Denis is an investor in startup companies and a board member of several small technology businesses across the world, as well as a business coach. He is a former director of McKinsey & Co, management consultants, and a former trustee of Sense International.

Denis obtained his MA in Economics from the University of Cambridge, followed by an MBA from Stanford University and qualified as a Chartered Accountant. He was elected to Council in 2010 and is a member of the Finance and Executive Committee. Denis is also a former parent.

Dr Valerie Udale - Chairman of Audit and Risk Committee and Council Health and Safety link

Valerie followed a career in corporate finance with teaching mathematics. She currently enjoys a range of professional interests from financial modelling consultancy, delivering corporate finance training and teaching in secondary school.

Valerie holds a D Phil in Developmental Neurobiology from the University of Oxford, a degree in Natural Sciences from the University of Cambridge, a PGCE from the University of Gloucestershire and a Diploma in Mathematics from the Open University.

Valerie was elected to Council in 2012. As well as being Chairman of the Audit and Risk Committee, she is a member of the Land and Buildings Committee and is the Health and Safety Link Council Member. Valerie is a current parent.

Mrs Fiona Weldin – Support Staff Representative

Fiona has worked in independent schools for 26 years. At College her posts included Head of Sixth Form, and Deputy Head, Admissions and Communications, as well as previous roles teaching English and as a housemistress. Fiona is currently Director of School Relationships with the Springboard Bursary Foundation.

Fiona was elected to Council in 2015 and is a member of the Audit and Risk Committee, as well as being the SEND Link Council Member.

Mrs Dilys Williams – Chairman of Investments Sub-Committee

Mrs Dilys Williams – Chairman of the Finance and Executive Committee and the Investments Sub-Committee

Dilys is an independent business adviser and consultant, having previously held positions as a partner and director in a national accountancy practice. She is a Chartered Accountant.

A former pupil, Dilys holds a Classics degree from the University of Cambridge. She was elected to Council in 2011 and is Chairman of both the Finance and Executive Committee and the Investments Sub-Committee.

Mrs Louise Williamson

Louise is a director of a multi-national facilities management company and has previously held directorships in several construction firms. She is a Chartered Engineer, a Fellow of the Institution of Civil Engineers and a Member of the British Institution of Facilities Management.

Louise gained her degree in Civil Engineering from City University, London and later completed the Business Leaders Programme at Cranfield School of Management. She was elected to Council in 2009. Louise serves on the Audit and Risk Committee and the Land and Buildings Committee.
